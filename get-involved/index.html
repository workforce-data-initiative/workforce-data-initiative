--- title: Get Involved ---

Data@Work is a major project of the University of Chicago's Center for Data Science and Public Policy, the Department of Labor, the National Skills Coalition, and a large set of community stakeholders. Community building continues to be a core component of the project: anyone can get involved and contributions are very welcome.

In order to make creating, using, and sharing workforce data as easy and frictionless as possible, we need concrete use cases and feedback from people working across government, industry, and academia:

  • Are you a researcher that needs specific skills data or tool to do your work more effectively?
  • Are you a developer working on an application to help people find jobs that our standards should support?

Let us know. We’d love to hear about it. For examples of data that's already been made available, see the data page. For examples of what has already been built, see the tools page. For information on where we’re going, see our roadmap.

We're also looking for volunteer "curators" interested in helping us create a set of high quality, useful skills to help us improve the skills data and associated algorithms.

Contact

If you're not sure where to start, there are lots of ways to get in contact with the core team members as well as the broader community around Data@Work

Forum

If you have a use case that you'd like to see Data@Work support, leave a note in the forum.

Chat

We also have a and open slack organization dedicated to technical discussion about Open Skills and Training Provider Outcomes Tools. Feel free to stop by and introduce yourself and join the conversation.

GitHub

All code developed in support of this project is hosted on the workforce data initiative organization on GitHub. Comments, forks, and pull requests welcome.

Newsletter

Sign up for our newsletter to receive updates on tooling or specification releases, events, and other news related to Data@Work.
